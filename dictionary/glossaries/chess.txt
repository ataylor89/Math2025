Combination:
(1) A tactic that consists of two or more moves

Endgame:
(1) The third phase of a chess game, when queens have been traded and queens are no longer on the board

Middlegame:
(1) The second phase of a chess game, where the players are "out of book"
(2) The second phase of a chess game, where the moves are no longer known in advance

Opening:
(1) The first phase of a chess game, where the moves are known in advance through memorization or study

Strategy:
(1) A plan
(2) The area of chess concerned with planning

Tactic:
(1) A short sequence of moves that yields an advantage or a winning advantage
